    Yellowstone National Park: Wildlife

    The American Bison has had a decreasing population due to lack of habitat and hunting from humans.

    Squirrels are a common rodent to see in Yellowstone. They are omnivores eating plans and small insects.

    An elk is about 4 or 5 feet tall and weigh around 600 pounds. You can tell the difference from a moose because their coat is much lighter.

    The grizzly bear is the largest bear in Yellowstone. Bears will try to avoid you so make sure to be noisy so they know your there.

    A moose stands at around 6 or 7 feet tall and weigh around 900 pounds. Although they are herbivores, they are very territorial and tourists should use extreme caution.

    Yaks are a form of cattle known for their long hair. They stay in a herd grazing on grass.
